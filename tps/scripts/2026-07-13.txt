The second is to watch the yen. If there's a single thread running through Japanese economic life, it's the value of the currency. A weak yen makes Japanese exports cheaper and more competitive abroad, which the big manufacturers love — but it makes everything Japan imports more expensive, and Japan imports a great deal, including most of its energy and much of its food. So a weak yen is a blessing for Toyota and a burden for a family buying groceries. When you understand that tension, an enormous amount of Japanese economic news suddenly makes sense. It's a country constantly balancing the interests of its great exporters against the cost of daily life for ordinary people.
