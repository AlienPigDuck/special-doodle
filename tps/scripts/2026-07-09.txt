A huge share of the world's crude passes through that narrow stretch of water between Iran and the Arabian Peninsula. When shipping there is threatened, the whole world feels it — and it did. WTI crude, the American benchmark, jumped to seventy-five dollars a barrel, its highest in two weeks, as traders priced in the real possibility that oil supplies get disrupted.

And here's the thing to keep in mind for Japan specifically: this is a country that imports nearly all of its energy, much of it from that very region. So when Hormuz gets tense, it's not an abstraction here. It ripples into everything — the price of gasoline, the cost of running a factory, the trade balance.
