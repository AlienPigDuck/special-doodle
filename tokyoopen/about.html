Tokyo Open

Architecture & Data Sources

What Is This?

Tokyo Open is a fully automated daily podcast that briefs listeners on the Japanese stock market before the morning session opens. Every episode is generated end-to-end by AI — no human writes, records, or edits anything. It runs automatically at 6:00 AM Japan Standard Time every day.

Two AI hosts — Alex (male, macro-focused) and Maya (female, stock-picker) — discuss what happened overnight in US markets, what that means for Tokyo when the bell rings, and any analyst rating changes or significant news from Japan's morning press.

Pipeline

Each episode is produced in five stages, taking roughly 10–15 minutes from start to finish.

1
Market Data Collection
Live prices, percentage changes, and after-hours moves are pulled from Yahoo Finance. This covers US indices, 10 sector ETFs, 34 key US stocks, and 41 key Japanese stocks from the previous Tokyo close. USD/JPY, crude oil, and gold are also fetched. Takes ~15 seconds.
2
News Collection
Two sources run in parallel. Six international RSS feeds are polled for articles from the past 14 hours. Simultaneously, a headless browser authenticates with a major Japanese news publication and scrapes the full morning newspaper edition — typically 120+ articles in Japanese spanning front page, markets, international, business, investment, and commodities sections.
3
News Digest (Claude Haiku)
All collected articles — including Japanese-language ones — are sent to Claude Haiku, which produces a structured six-section briefing: Market Movers, Macro & FX, Sector Themes, Key Events & Earnings, Surprises & Risks, and Analyst Ratings. The Analyst Ratings section specifically extracts broker upgrades, downgrades, and price-target changes from major houses (Daiwa, Nomura, Jefferies, Mizuho, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, and others). Takes ~10 seconds.
4
Script Generation (Claude Opus)
Market data, the news briefing, and a US→Japan correlation map are sent to Claude Opus, which writes a 600–800 word two-host dialogue. The script cites articles from Japan's morning press by name where relevant, and calls out analyst rating changes with the broker and new target. Takes ~30 seconds.
5
Voice Synthesis & Publish
The script is split into individual speaker lines and synthesised using Google Cloud Text-to-Speech — en-GB-Journey-D for Alex (British male) and en-GB-Journey-F for Maya (British female). On alternate days, ElevenLabs voices are used instead: Christie (male) voices Alex, and Isla voices Maya. All segments are stitched together with silence gaps using ffmpeg and the final MP3 is published to this site. Takes ~8 minutes.

Automation

The entire pipeline runs on GitHub Actions — no dedicated server required. A scheduled trigger fires at 6:00 AM Japan Standard Time every day. The generated MP3 is pushed directly to this GitHub Pages site via the GitHub API, overwriting latest.mp3. The episode date metadata is updated in episode.json, which the player reads to display the correct date.

All market data is sourced from public APIs. News is pulled from publicly available RSS feeds and a licensed Japanese news publication. This project has no affiliation with any of the companies, exchanges, or publications mentioned.
